Oil Pitch : The Massive Undertaking of Qatar 2022 World Cup

Oleh: Achmad Prayogi Rafsanjani

In these recent years as a sports fans, you might’ve taken notice of big steps taken by gulf countries by the like of Qatar in sporting business such as bringing famous athletes to their countries, organizing world class sporting event, and building big infrastructure projects even though it would be only used for just one event. In this article writers want to highlight the biggest examples of massive effort taken by these formerly known for fossil-fuel producer to diversificate into sports industry as one of their efforts to attract tourist.

Qatar 2022: Building Football From Scratch

As tradition before a world cup was held the next year in south Africa, FIFA held a bidding for countries whom wanted to host two next world cup event after the 2014 in Brazil (2018 & 2022). On the 2022 world cup bidding, an unusual bid came from Qatar, a small monarch country bordering Persian Gulf to the north and surrounded by Saudi Arabia to the south. Prior to the 2009 bidding Qatar had organize and host the 2006 asian games proving they could hold their weight in hosting international even continental sporting events, this became the main argument especially for Asian Football Confederation (AFC) President Muhammad Bin Hammam. Sepp Blater, FIFA president at the time was also intrigued by the idea of bringing the World Cup to the middle east. After one year process of voting it was confirmed Qatar will be hosting the 2022 World cup event, it was a start of Qatar infrastructure Spending spree.

Qatari state built not only the footballing infrastructure such as Abu Ehoud Stadium, Lusail Stadium, Khalifa Staidum and four other stadium around the country that held all the matches played but also Lusail city from groundup with capacity of 250,000 people to host players, fans and officials. Existing infrastructure improvement was also made to accommodate tourist coming tom capital Doha with Doha tram, Airport Expansion and accommodation spread throughout the country. It is estimated that Qatari state spent around 730 Billion in Qatari Riyal or approximately around 220 Billion US Dollar, Largest spending ever by world cup hosting Nation. While only contributing around 6 billion dollar revenue in one momth the event was held.

So the question comes, why did they do it? They did not earn a profit even loss a great amount of money, resources, and a decade of infrastructure project time just for a month of euphoria and festivities of the biggest footballing event. Simply, it was the biggest publicity opportunity to put Qatar on the world attention for an entire month in a way of rebranding from only an fossil fuel producing country to world sporting destination. Not only since that, Qatar have been hosting more and more sporting event such as Formula one Doha Grand Prix held annually that attracts many of the richest person to come watch and spent their money for a weekend, Women World Cup and 2023 Asian Cup. This is only the start of Qatar investment in Sporting industry as world shifted from fossil fuel to renewable energy.

To close this article, writer wanted to remind readers the other costs that comes with the expense for this 2022 world cup to be completed. Hundreds of thousands migrant worker imported mainly from south asia to Qatar as construction and service workers in hope of better pay and employment just to be treated by their hosts almost like slave because of sham contracts. Poor housing, treatment and safety working regulation caused thousands of dead of these workers by their negligent employer. Many cannot escape because of their passport is being held by their employer.

These are not statistic but human lives lost and continue increasing, all of them are someone’s family member, sons, daughter, brother, sister and even mother or father of a child. The need of proper, fairer, and safer worker regulations are urgently needed now than ever before more lives lost not only in Qatar but around Middle East, let us remember the lives were lost for these ambition and prevent more by pressuring these country lawmakers to push for workers safety and welfare.
